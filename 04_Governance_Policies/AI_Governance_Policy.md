# AI Governance Policy

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish governance requirements for the development, deployment, operation, monitoring and oversight of the EthosSutra Enterprise Copilot.

This policy defines the principles, responsibilities and control expectations necessary to support responsible AI practices, effective risk management and regulatory compliance throughout the AI system lifecycle.

---

# Scope

This policy applies to:

- EthosSutra Enterprise Copilot
- Governance documentation
- AI system development activities
- AI system operations
- Human oversight activities
- Third-party AI services
- AI-related risk management activities

This policy applies to all individuals involved in the design, operation, monitoring and governance of the AI system.

---

# Governance Objectives

The objectives of this policy are to:

- Promote responsible AI practices
- Establish accountability
- Support risk-informed decision making
- Protect information assets
- Enable regulatory compliance
- Improve transparency and auditability
- Ensure appropriate human oversight

---

# Governance Principles

## Principle 1: Accountability

Ownership and accountability must be assigned for all AI systems, governance activities and risk management processes.

All AI systems must have identified business and technical owners.

---

## Principle 2: Human Oversight

Human oversight must be maintained throughout the AI system lifecycle.

AI-generated outputs must support human decision-making and must not replace human judgment for governance, compliance or risk-related decisions.

---

## Principle 3: Transparency

System architecture, governance processes, risk assessments and monitoring activities must be documented and available for review.

Governance decisions should be traceable and auditable.

---

## Principle 4: Risk Management

AI-related risks must be identified, assessed, documented and monitored throughout the system lifecycle.

Risk treatment activities must be proportionate to the level of risk identified.

---

## Principle 5: Security and Data Protection

Information processed by the AI system must be protected through appropriate technical and organizational controls.

Data handling activities must comply with applicable policies and standards.

---

## Principle 6: Continuous Monitoring

AI systems must be monitored to evaluate performance, reliability, incidents and emerging risks.

Monitoring activities should support ongoing governance oversight.

---

# Governance Roles and Responsibilities

## AI Governance Lead

Responsibilities include:

- Governance program oversight
- Policy management
- Risk management oversight
- Governance reporting
- Audit support

---

## System Owner

Responsibilities include:

- Operational accountability
- Resource allocation
- Business alignment
- Compliance with governance requirements

---

## Technical Owner

Responsibilities include:

- Technical implementation
- System maintenance
- Security controls
- Technical documentation

---

## Users

Responsibilities include:

- Appropriate system usage
- Compliance with acceptable use requirements
- Reporting issues and incidents

---

# Governance Requirements

The following artefacts must be maintained:

- AI Use Case Intake Form
- AI System Register
- AI Risk Assessment
- Risk Register
- Human Oversight Plan
- Model Card
- Testing Strategy
- Monitoring Plan
- Vendor Risk Assessment
- Control Library
- Audit Evidence Pack

---

# Risk Management Requirements

All AI systems must:

- Undergo formal risk assessment
- Maintain a risk register
- Implement risk treatment activities
- Monitor residual risks
- Review risks periodically

---

# Human Oversight Requirements

Human oversight must be applied to:

- Governance recommendations
- Compliance interpretations
- Risk assessments
- Audit conclusions
- Control implementation decisions

Human reviewers remain accountable for decisions supported by the AI system.

---

# Third-Party Governance

Third-party vendors supporting the AI system must be assessed for:

- Security
- Reliability
- Compliance
- Operational resilience
- Service dependencies

Vendor assessments must be reviewed periodically.

---

# Monitoring Requirements

Monitoring activities must include:

- System availability
- Response quality
- User feedback
- Security events
- Incident reporting
- Vendor performance

Monitoring records should be retained as governance evidence.

---

# Incident Management

AI-related incidents must be:

- Reported
- Investigated
- Documented
- Resolved
- Reviewed for lessons learned

Significant incidents must be escalated through governance processes.

---

# Policy Exceptions

Exceptions to this policy must be documented, reviewed and formally approved by the AI Governance Lead.

Approved exceptions must include documented rationale and compensating controls where applicable.

---

# Compliance and Review

Compliance with this policy is mandatory.

The policy should be reviewed annually or following significant changes to:

- System architecture
- Regulatory requirements
- Risk profile
- Operating environment
