# Risk Assessment

## Purpose

The Risk Assessment process identifies, evaluates and manages risks associated with the EthosSutra Enterprise Copilot throughout its lifecycle.

The objective is to ensure that AI-related risks are understood, documented, monitored and mitigated through appropriate governance controls and human oversight mechanisms.

Risk management is a core component of responsible AI governance and supports informed decision-making, regulatory compliance and operational resilience.

## Risk Domains

The EthosSutra Enterprise Copilot risk assessment considers multiple risk categories including:

### Technical Risks

* Hallucinations
* Model performance degradation
* Prompt injection attacks
* Retrieval failures
* System availability issues

### Data Risks

* Data leakage
* Unauthorized disclosure
* Knowledge base inaccuracies
* Data quality issues

### Compliance Risks

* Regulatory non-compliance
* Governance control failures
* Documentation deficiencies

### Operational Risks

* Process failures
* Monitoring gaps
* Incident management weaknesses

### Third-Party Risks

* Foundation model dependency
* Cloud provider dependency
* Vendor service disruptions

### Reputational Risks

* Inaccurate governance guidance
* Loss of stakeholder trust
* Public credibility concerns

## Governance Principle

All AI systems must undergo risk assessment before production deployment.

Risk assessments must be reviewed periodically and updated whenever significant changes occur to the AI system, architecture, data sources, vendors or intended use cases.

## Expected Deliverables
