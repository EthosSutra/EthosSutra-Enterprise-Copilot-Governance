# Testing and Validation

## Purpose

The Testing and Validation process establishes the methodology used to evaluate the performance, reliability, accuracy and governance effectiveness of the EthosSutra Enterprise Copilot.

The objective is to ensure that the AI system performs as intended, operates within acceptable risk tolerances and remains aligned with governance requirements throughout its lifecycle.

Testing activities provide evidence that the system has been appropriately assessed before deployment and continues to operate effectively after implementation.

## Key Outcomes

* Functional requirements validated
* Governance controls tested
* Human oversight controls verified
* Hallucination risk assessed
* Technical controls evaluated
* Deployment readiness established

## Governance Objectives

The Testing and Validation process supports:

* Responsible AI practices
* Risk management
* Governance assurance
* Compliance readiness
* Operational resilience
* Audit readiness

## Testing Principles

### Risk-Based Testing

Testing activities should focus on risks identified through the AI Risk Assessment process.

### Repeatability

Testing procedures should be documented and repeatable.

### Traceability

Testing results should be linked to requirements, controls and risks.

### Independence

Testing results should be reviewed by appropriate stakeholders before approval.

### Continuous Improvement

Testing outcomes should inform future system improvements and risk mitigation activities.

## Testing Scope

Testing activities may include:

* Functional testing
* Governance control testing
* Hallucination testing
* Retrieval quality testing
* Security testing
* User acceptance testing
* Performance testing

## Success Criteria

The testing program is considered successful when:

* Functional requirements are satisfied
* Governance controls operate effectively
* Hallucination risks are understood and managed
* Security controls are validated
* Human oversight controls are demonstrated
* Deployment approval is granted

AI systems should demonstrate evidence of effective testing and validation before being relied upon for governance, risk and compliance activities.

Testing is not a one-time activity and should continue throughout the system lifecycle.
